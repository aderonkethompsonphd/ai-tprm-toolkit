## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes [Organisation Name]'s approach to managing cybersecurity, data protection, sustainability, and compliance risks arising from third-party vendors, suppliers, and service providers. It implements the requirements of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls 5.19–5.22, NIS2 Directive Article 21(3), GDPR Article 28, and [add applicable regulations].

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all third-party relationships involving:
- Access to [Organisation]'s information systems or networks
- Processing of personal data on behalf of [Organisation]
- Provision of services critical to [Organisation]'s operations
- Supply of products integrated into [Organisation]'s infrastructure

---

## 3. Risk Tier Classification

All vendors are classified as Critical, High, Medium, or Low based on their potential impact on [Organisation]'s operations and data. See: `governance/vendor_risk_tier_model.md`.

---

## 4. Vendor Assessment Requirements

| Tier | Pre-onboarding | Questionnaire | DPA | Reassessment |
|------|---------------|---------------|-----|--------------|
| Critical | Full due diligence + sanctions | Full (60+ Qs) | Mandatory | 6 months |
| High | Standard due diligence | Extended (40 Qs) | If processing PD | 12 months |
| Medium | Sanctions check | Standard (20 Qs) | If processing PD | 24 months |
| Low | Sanctions check | KYC only (5 Qs) | Not required | 36 months |

---

## 5. Contractual Requirements

All supplier agreements must include:
- Information security requirements aligned with NIS2 Article 21(2)
- Incident notification obligation (within 24 hours of discovery)
- Right to audit
- Data processing agreement (where personal data is involved)
- Data deletion/return at contract end
- Sub-processor disclosure and approval requirement
- Exit strategy and transition provisions

---

## 6. Ongoing Monitoring

- All vendors reassessed per the schedule above
- Reassessment triggered immediately on: vendor security incident, material change in service, change in vendor ownership, intelligence indicating compromise
- KPI dashboard reviewed monthly by TPRM Lead and quarterly by management

---

## 7. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| TPRM Lead | Own and operate the TPRM programme, maintain vendor register, conduct assessments |
| Vendor Owner | Internal business contact responsible for each vendor relationship |
| Legal | Execute and review DPAs and supplier agreements |
| IT Security | Provision and revoke vendor access, monitor vendor security alerts |
| Procurement | Enforce TPRM requirements in procurement process |
| Management | Approve TPRM programme, review quarterly KPI dashboard |
